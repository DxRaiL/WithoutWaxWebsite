“Without Wax”

Some Background on the Business Philosophy

According to legend, sculptors in ancient Rome and Greece had great pride in the making of the vases and pots they would sell in the market place. Therefore, chipped or cracked pieces would be thrown out, and only perfect pieces would be sold. However, some sculptors began using wax to cover imperfections and painted over it. The deception was only seen by holding the items up to the scrutiny of light to see the faint glow. The vases and pots that were without wax were called sine cera (sincere) to show they were genuine and honest.

Our mission is to provide services "without wax" that will show our sincerity when held up to the light.
